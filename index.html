Simran came in with a clear mindset: "I want to explore non-clinical. It's not necessary to restrict ourselves to one zone." No deadline, no desperation. Just the rare self-awareness to ask the right question before time ran out.

She listed oral medicine, pharmacology, then mentioned oral surgery — and her eyes lit up. Not because she wants to be a surgeon, but because of what she said next without thinking: "Every case is different. No two extractions are the same. It never gets boring."

That wasn't someone describing a clinical skill. That was someone describing how her mind works — she loves variety, problem-solving under uncertainty, environments where every day brings something new. That is an analytical personality trait.
